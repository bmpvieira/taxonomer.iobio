Human serum sample positive for Zaire ebolavirus

 This will start analysis of the first ~40k reads of published RNA-Seq data of a human serum sample positive for Zaire ebolavirus from the current outbreak in Sierra Leone, West Africa. Selection of viral reads demonstrates ~75% of the viral reads being classified as Zaire Ebolavirus. Per published data, this sample had an ebolavirus load of 1.61E+09 copies/ml by qPCR. The associated publication is here: http://www.sciencemag.org/content/345/6202/1369.long The data is from: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sra/?term=SRR1553464

16S rRNA (V4) amplicon data from a stool sample of a child

 http://taxonomer.iobio.io/?url=https://s3.amazonaws.com/iobio/ERR501052_1.fastq 16S rRNA (V4) amplicon data from a stool sample of a child enrolled in this study: http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v510/n7505/full/nature13421.html Virtually all reads are binned as bacterial and this allows you to explore a sample with diverse bacterial flora. The data is from: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sra/?term=ERR501052

 This is published data from a shotgun metagenomics study of a serum sample from a patient with hemorrhagic fever of unknown etiology (Democratic Republic of Congo).
