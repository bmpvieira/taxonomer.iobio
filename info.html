Sample Datasets

Human serum sample positive for Zaire ebolavirus

 This will start analysis of the first ~40k reads of published RNA-Seq data of a human serum sample positive for Zaire ebolavirus from the current outbreak in Sierra Leone, West Africa. Selection of viral reads demonstrates ~75% of the viral reads being classified as Zaire Ebolavirus. Per published data, this sample had an ebolavirus load of 1.61E+09 copies/ml by qPCR. Metagenomics study of a serum sample from a patient with hemorrhagic fever

 This is published data from a shotgun metagenomics study of a serum sample from a patient with hemorrhagic fever of unknown etiology (Democratic Republic of Congo). This data resulted in discovery of a novel rhabdovirus (Bas-Congo virus) as the cause of this patientâ€™s symptoms and a related outbreak. See publication here. Selection of the viral reads shows ~45% of reads being classified as Bas-Congo virus. The sample was contaminated with human rotavirus (see publication), which represents ~10% of the viral reads. See data here.
