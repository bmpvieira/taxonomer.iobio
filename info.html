Sample Datasets

Human Serum Sample Positive for Zaire Ebolavirus, Liberia (RNA-Seq)

 RNA-Seq data of a serum sample from a patient in Sierra Leone, West Africa, who tested positive for Ebolavirus by PCR (1.61E+09 copies/ml). Selection of viral reads demonstrates ~75% of the reads being classified as Zaire Ebolavirus (see below).


Associated publication.

Intestinal Microbiota Profile from a Malnourished Child, Bangladesh (16S Amplicon Sequencing)

 16S rRNA (V4) amplicon sequencing data from a stool sample of a malnourished child in Dhaka, Bangladesh. Virtually all reads are binned as bacterial and can be explored by selecting different taxonomic ranks in the graph.

Human Serum Sample from a Patient with Hemorrhagic Fever Positive for Bas-Congo Virus, Democratic Republic of Congo (RNA-Seq)

 RNA-Seq data of a serum sample from a patient with hemorrhagic fever of unknown etiology (Democratic Republic of Congo). This data resulted in discovery of a novel rhabdovirus (Bas-Congo virus) as the cause of this patientâ€™s symptoms and a related outbreak. Selection of the viral reads shows ~45% of reads being classified as Bas-Congo virus. The sample was contaminated with human rotavirus (see publication), which represents ~10% of the viral reads.
